Our Story

An 1890 Victorian in The Nations, brought back to life.

When we found this house, it was the most forgotten Victorian in the neighborhood. A century-old beauty on a corner lot, in the part of Nashville locals call The Nations. Original 1890 mantels, gorgeous crystal chandeliers, and show-stopping twelve-foot pocket doors with brass thumb-pulls.

"We didn't want to flip this house. We wanted to keep its soul."

The decision was easy: not to rebuild the house, but to restore all the charm and beauty of the past. The fireplace tile stayed. The pine floors stayed. We added crystal chandeliers, emerald velvet chesterfields, a brass leopard pedestal because we couldn't help ourselves, and the kind of bedding you sleep eleven hours in.

During the renovation, neighbors stopped by daily to watch the progress. Now they stop to take pictures.

— NooknKey

Where you'll be

The Nations,
Nashville.

A former industrial corner of West Nashville, now the city's quietest food and design scene. Close to everything; insulated from the noise.

~8 min drive

Broadway

Nashville's most famous street. Honky tonks four stories deep, live country music from open till last call, neon you can read from space. Eight minutes from your front door.
~5 min walk

51st Ave Dining

Steps from your door: Frothy Monkey for the morning espresso, 51st Deli for the sandwich locals send out-of-towners to, Jeni's Splendid Ice Creams for the sweet break, and Nicky's Coal Fired — featured on Diners, Drive-Ins & Dives — for dinner. The best block in The Nations, on foot.
~15 min drive

BNA Airport

Fifteen minutes from baggage claim to your front door, on a straight line down I-40. The whole point of a direct booking is no friction — this is where it starts.
